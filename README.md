# 📊 Global Cancer Patient Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Project Overview
This project presents an end-to-end data analysis of global cancer patient data (2015–2024) using Power BI.  
The objective is to identify key factors influencing patient outcomes, particularly **survival years** and **severity score**.

The dashboard provides insights into how **age, cancer stage, treatment cost, and lifestyle factors** impact cancer progression and survival.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze trends in cancer patient outcomes over time  
- Identify key factors affecting **survival and severity**  
- Compare patterns across **age groups, cancer types, and regions**  
- Understand the impact of **lifestyle and environmental risk factors**  
- Build an interactive dashboard for data-driven insights  

---

## 📁 Dataset
- Global cancer patient dataset (2015–2024)  
- ~50,000 patient records  
- Includes:
  - Demographics (Age, Gender, Country)
  - Cancer details (Type, Stage)
  - Metrics (Survival Years, Severity Score, Treatment Cost)
  - Risk factors (Smoking, Alcohol, Obesity, Air Pollution, Genetic Risk)

---

## 📊 Dashboard Features

### 🔹 Trend Analysis

---

### 🔹 Age-Based Analysis
- Patient distribution across age groups

---

### 🔹 Cancer & Stage Analysis
- Survival trends across cancer stages

---

### 🔹 Heatmaps
- Severity across countries and cancer types  
- Survival across age groups and cancer stages  

---

### 🔹 Risk Factor Analysis
- Comparison of smoking, alcohol, obesity, pollution, and genetic risk  
- Highlights combined lifestyle impact on severity  

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

- Survival rates declined until 2018 and improved afterward  
- Cancer stage is the **most critical factor** affecting survival  
- Older age groups show **higher severity and lower survival**  
- Treatment costs are rising but **do not consistently improve outcomes**  
- Lifestyle factors collectively contribute to increased severity  

---

## 🧠 Notable Finding

> Despite a slight improvement in Stage IV survival in 2018, overall survival declined due to a higher proportion of late-stage cases.
